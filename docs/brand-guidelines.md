# Bearlin Brand Guidelines

Bearlin is an original German-learning product for English-speaking learners. Its product interface and learning support use English; German is reserved for target-language readings, vocabulary, examples, and necessary linguistic forms. It may use familiar graded-reader interaction patterns, but it must not reuse Du Chinese names, logos, illustrations, screenshots, stories, translations, recordings, or proprietary copy.

## Identity

- **Name:** Bearlin
- **Concept:** A friendly Berlin bear guiding learners through progressively richer German reading.
- **Voice:** Clear, encouraging, curious, and concise.
- **Primary phrase:** “Read German. Learn naturally.”
- **Mascot direction:** An original geometric bear/book mark. Do not trace or imitate Du Chinese’s character-book logo.

## Visual direction

- **Primary:** Bearlin Red `#C92A3A`
- **Primary dark:** `#9F1F2C`
- **Accent:** Linden Gold `#F2B84B`
- **Success:** `#2F9E77`
- **Ink:** `#202124`
- **Paper:** `#FFFDFC`
- **Muted surface:** `#F4F1EF`
- **Dark surface:** `#171719`
- **Shape language:** Rounded cards, restrained shadows, clear spacing, and compact level chips.
- **Illustration direction:** Original flat editorial scenes inspired by German daily life, geography, folklore in the public domain, and contemporary culture. Mock covers should use abstract shapes and typography rather than copied artwork.

## Content originality

- Write all mock German passages plus their English translations, dictionary entries, exercises, and grammar explanations specifically for Bearlin.
- Public-domain themes may be adapted only with newly written text and original cover art.
- Use fabricated people and situations for contemporary stories.
- Use only bundled, self-created, or properly licensed audio and imagery; track licenses in `assets/ATTRIBUTIONS.md` when required.
- Do not claim “native speaker audio” while mock or synthesized audio is in use.

## Implementation guardrails

1. Build original components and layouts from the Bearlin design tokens rather than pixel-copying screenshots.
2. Use only original mock catalog titles, descriptions, text, metadata, and covers.
3. Keep Du Chinese references in research documentation only.
4. Verify every non-code asset’s origin before committing it.
5. Preserve the existing Bearlin app name, scheme, and package identity unless explicitly requested otherwise.
